# Content Writing Capability

- Match the client's tone and brand voice — check PROJECT.md for client context, audience, and any stated style preferences before writing
- Article structure: hook (open with a tension or surprising claim) → context (why this matters now) → body (H2/H3 sections, one idea per section) → conclusion (synthesis, not summary) → CTA (single, specific action)
- Cite all factual claims with sources — include URLs, report names, and publication dates inline or in a Sources section; never state statistics without attribution
- Produce draft-ready output — no "insert X here", "add quote here", or "expand this section" placeholders; every section must be complete
- Investor materials: lead with traction and evidence (metrics, growth, customers), then vision and market size, then the ask — confidence and specificity over aspiration
- Outreach sequences: open with a personalized observation about the recipient or their company, state the value proposition in one sentence, close with a single clear CTA; one ask per email
- Market research: cite multiple independent sources for each finding, quantify market size and growth rates where possible, explicitly distinguish between verified facts and analyst opinion
- Multi-format content: adapt voice and length per platform — Twitter/X under 280 characters, LinkedIn longer-form with line breaks, newsletter with subject line and preview text
- Presentations: one idea per slide, 3–5 bullets maximum, include speaker notes with the narrative thread
